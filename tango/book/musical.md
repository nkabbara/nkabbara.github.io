Point: I'm not naturally musical

Somewhere around the early 90s when we lived in Ghana, my dad drove me and my brothers to go play polo at The Shangri-La. He was playing a popular song by the Egyptian virtuoso Mohamed Abdel Wahab on the radio. My brothers with their mouths transformed into megaphones where blasting out the lyrics and humming the tune in full right-left stereo directly behind my head. Pulling that decades old image back into the present, I can see how the vibrations entered my brothers' ears and possessed them. They were not with us in the car, they where enraptured by the music and the singer. At 8 years old the lyrics would have made no sense to them, but that didn't matter. I remember my annoyance building up not only because of the loudness of the music, but also at how it bounced off my ears in painful deflections. It did not enter my body, instead it stopped at my ear drum and refused to take the journey into my soul. I turned around from the front seat to face my two brothers and screamed the following lyrics: "SHUT THE FUCK UP!". My dad disrupted the start of my ballad with "They are entraced by the music, don't break the spell." and flicked my head to make sure that these words actually entered deeper into my head beyond my eardrums. Fast forward to college to the time when we lived together in the same house. I started taking djembe drumming lessons as hobby and about 3 months in, one of my brothers' friends asked me to play what I had been learning.
